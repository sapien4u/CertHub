Module 7 · Cheat Sheets

Exam-day tips

Practical strategy for GH-300.

Before the exam

  • Confirm the current format, question count, passing score, and objective weightings on the official page.
  • Ensure your environment meets the online-proctoring requirements (ID, quiet room, webcam, stable connection).
  • Do a final pass of the cheat sheets and your weakest domain in the question bank.

Time management

  • ~90 minutes; keep a steady pace and don't over-invest in any single item.
  • Flag uncertain questions and return to them.
  • Answer every question — there's no benefit to leaving blanks.

Reading questions

  • Watch for "select all that apply" — multiple correct answers.
  • Note qualifiers: "best", "most", "first", "always", "never".
  • Eliminate clearly wrong options first, then choose among the rest.

Reliable heuristics

  • Human-in-the-loop wins. Options requiring review/validation/testing are usually right for Responsible-AI.
  • Org features = Business/Enterprise. Audit logs, policies, exclusions, metrics, indemnity are not on the individual plan.
  • Knowledge bases = Enterprise. Deep github.com integration = Enterprise.
  • Context is the fix. If Copilot output is generic/wrong, better context/prompt is the usual remedy.
  • Beware absolutes. "Copilot guarantees…" is typically wrong — it assists, it doesn't guarantee.

Common traps

⚠Common Mistake
  • Choosing an option that removes the human from the loop.
  • Assigning enterprise-admin features to individual plans.
  • Believing Copilot guarantees correctness, security, or originality.
  • Confusing prompt files (reusable prompts) with custom instructions (always-on rules).
★Best Practice

If two options look right, prefer the one that reflects responsible use (review, validation, appropriate plan/control) over the one that maximises speed or automation.

Good luck — you've got this.
