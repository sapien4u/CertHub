Module 5 · Enterprise Features

Plans & feature comparison

The plans domain is one of the largest on GH-300. You must be able to compare Copilot Free/Individual (Pro), Copilot Business, and Copilot Enterprise, and match capabilities to each.

The plans at a glance

Capability Free / Pro (Individual) Business Enterprise
Inline completions & chat Yes (Free has limits) Yes Yes
Managed via organization No Yes Yes
Policy & content exclusions Limited Yes Yes
Code not used to train models Pro: yes Yes Yes
IP indemnity (filter on) No Yes Yes
Audit logs No Yes Yes
Usage/engagement metrics No Yes Yes
github.com deep integration No Limited Yes
Knowledge bases No No Yes
Copilot in PRs (summaries, etc.) Limited Varies Yes (fuller)
ℹNote

Exact entitlements evolve. Focus on the shape: Individual = personal productivity; Business = org management, policy, security, indemnity; Enterprise = Business + deep platform integration and knowledge bases.

Individual (Free & Pro)

For a single developer. Copilot Free offers a limited monthly allowance of completions/chat; Copilot Pro is the paid individual plan with full editor features. No organization-level administration, policy management, audit logs, or indemnity.

Copilot Business

Adds the organization layer: centralised license management, policy controls, content exclusions, audit logs, usage metrics, and IP indemnity (with the duplication filter enabled). Your code is not used to train the models. This is the baseline for most companies.

Copilot Enterprise

Everything in Business plus deep GitHub-platform integration: Copilot on github.com, repository-aware chat, knowledge bases built from your docs, richer PR features, and (where offered) fine-tuned/organizational context. Best when the org lives on GitHub and wants Copilot to reason over its own knowledge.

Choosing a plan

Need Plan
Solo dev, personal projects Free / Pro
Company: manage licences, policy, security, indemnity Business
Deep github.com integration + org knowledge bases Enterprise
✓Exam Tip

Two high-yield lines: audit logs, policy management, content exclusions, and IP indemnity start at Business; knowledge bases and deep github.com/PR integration are Enterprise. Individual plans have none of the org-management features.

⚠Common Mistake

Attributing org-admin features (audit logs, policies, exclusions, metrics) to the individual plan. Those require Business or Enterprise.
