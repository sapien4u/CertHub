Module 5 · Enterprise Features

Copilot Spaces & Spark

The January 2026 exam revision explicitly names two newer capabilities: Copilot Spaces (curated context for grounded answers) and GitHub Spark (build apps from natural language). Both extend Copilot beyond inline coding.

Copilot Spaces

A Copilot Space is a curated, reusable bundle of context — code, docs, notes, and other references — that you assemble so Copilot Chat gives grounded, higher-quality answers for a specific topic, project, or task.

  • You choose what goes into the Space (repos, files, free-text instructions, links).
  • Chat then reasons within that curated context, reducing generic or off-target answers.
  • Spaces are shareable, so a team gets consistent, grounded responses instead of each person re-explaining the same background.

Think of a Space as "the briefing packet" you'd hand a new teammate, made available to Copilot.

Space: "Billing Service"
  Context: billing repo, the data model doc, the PCI checklist, "we use MediatR + EF Core"
  Result: Chat answers about billing use the right patterns, files, and constraints.
★Best Practice

Keep a Space focused and current. Curate the few references that matter for the topic rather than dumping everything — relevance beats volume for grounding.

GitHub Spark

GitHub Spark lets you build and deploy small full-stack apps from natural-language descriptions, iterating conversationally. You describe what you want; Spark generates the app, and you refine it through prompts.

  • Aimed at quickly turning an idea into a working, deployable app.
  • Lowers the barrier for prototypes, internal tools, and PM/maker use cases.
  • You still review, test, and secure what it produces before relying on it.
✓Exam Tip

Exam cues: "curated, shareable context for grounded chat answers" → Copilot Spaces; "build a full-stack app from a natural-language description" → GitHub Spark.

⚠Common Mistake

Treating a Spark-generated app or a Space-grounded answer as production-ready without review. Grounding and generation improve quality — they don't remove the need for testing, security review, and human accountability.

How they relate to plans

Availability of Spaces, Spark, and similar capabilities depends on plan tier and organization policy, and some features may be in preview. Confirm current availability for your plan before relying on them for the exam or in production.

⚠Warning

Preview features can change. The exam focuses mostly on generally available features but may include commonly used preview capabilities — know what each does conceptually.
