Module 4 · Responsible AI

Responsible AI overview

Responsible AI is a core GH-300 domain. It covers the risks inherent in generative AI and the practices — above all, human oversight — that keep Copilot use safe, fair, and accountable.

Key risks

Hallucinations

Copilot can produce output that is confident but wrong: nonexistent APIs, invented parameters, plausible-but-incorrect logic. This stems directly from its probabilistic, generative nature.

Bias and fairness

Because models learn from large public datasets, they can reflect biases present in that data — in naming, assumptions, examples, or comments. Outputs should be reviewed for fairness and inclusivity.

Over-reliance / automation bias

The tendency to trust automated output too readily. The more fluent the suggestion, the easier it is to skip scrutiny — which is exactly when errors slip through.

Security and quality

Generated code may contain vulnerabilities or poor practices learned from training data. It is not pre-verified for your context.

The human-in-the-loop principle

The unifying answer across this domain: the developer is accountable. Copilot assists; humans decide. Every suggestion must be reviewed, tested, and validated before it ships.

✓Exam Tip

When a Responsible-AI question offers an option involving human review, validation, or developer accountability, it is very often the correct answer. Copilot augments developers; it does not replace their judgement or responsibility.

Practices that mitigate the risks

  • Review everything Copilot produces before accepting or merging.
  • Test generated code, including edge cases and security-sensitive paths.
  • Scan for vulnerabilities (SAST, dependency scanning, secret scanning).
  • Verify APIs, packages, and licences actually exist and are appropriate.
  • Watch for bias in naming, examples, and assumptions.
  • Keep humans accountable for decisions, especially in high-stakes code.
★Best Practice

Adopt a "trust but verify" stance as a team norm: Copilot output enters the same review, test, and security gates as any other code — no exceptions because "the AI wrote it."

⚠Common Mistake

Framing Copilot as an authority. It is a probabilistic assistant. Treating its output as vetted truth is the root cause of most Responsible-AI failures.
